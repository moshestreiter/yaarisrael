Yaarisrael: Regenerating Israel's Native Forests

Yaarisrael is a nonprofit afforestation initiative based in the Beit She'an Valley. We grow native Israeli trees and plants in our own nursery and plant ultra-dense, biodiverse Miyawaki-style forests to combat desertification, restore habitat, and cool the land.

Why a Native Miyawaki Forest?

The Miyawaki method uses multi-layered, ultra-dense planting of native species to rapidly regenerate forest ecosystems. In Israel’s Beit She’an Valley, we face extreme heat, low rainfall, and land degradation. Our forests act as climate buffers, biodiversity oases, and cultural assets.

Native Species for the Beit She’an Valley

  • Ziziphus spina-christi – Hardy, drought-tolerant, shade provider
  • Tamarix jordanis – Salt-tolerant, suited to lowland soils
  • Ceratonia siliqua – Carob tree, nitrogen-fixer, edible pods
  • Pistacia atlantica – Deep-rooted, resilient, keystone tree
  • Quercus ithaburensis – Native oak, strong ecological presence
  • Acacia raddiana – Arid-zone nitrogen-fixer, wildlife-friendly
  • Retama raetam – White Broom, flowering shrub for drylands
  • Capparis spinosa – Caper bush, iconic and edible
  • Rhamnus palaestinus – Buckthorn, bird-attracting berries
  • Calicotome villosa – Thorny cover for seedling protection
  • Salvia fruticosa – Sage, pollinator-attractor, fragrant herb
  • Sarcopoterium spinosum – Groundcover, erosion control
  • Stipa capensis – Feather grass, early succession species
  • Echinops adenocaulos – Blue thistle, pollinator-friendly

Yaarisrael Business Plan

Phase 1: Seed Procurement & Nursery Establishment

- Collect and purchase seeds of native trees and plants
- Construct a shaded nursery and irrigation system (20 x 60 ft)
- Propagate 2,500–3,000 seedlings per cycle
- Use the nursery for community education and volunteer engagement

Phase 2: Pilot Miyawaki Forest

- Plant a 1-dunam forest using 25+ native species
- Install irrigation, mulch, and fencing
- Maintain for 2–3 years to ensure forest independence
- Document biodiversity and climate impact
